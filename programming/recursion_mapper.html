Recursion Mapper

Recursion Mapper is designed to find the shortest route between two nodes. It can look at the number of connections, the distance to travel or it can consider the weighting of different connections.

It has three ways to render the network. The first is static, where all nodes and connections are the same size, no matter what their weight is. The second is dynamic, which makes higher weighted nodes and connections bigger. The last, and currently least stable, is textured mode. This makes nodes behave like settlements, with different textures for different sizes, and makes connections like roads, which have a dynamically generated number of lanes based on their weight.

I also introduced themes, which style the textured mode differently. These are all stored in files which are editable by the user. For example, there could be a desert theme, a winter theme or a rural theme.
